Panic Attacks: What to Do and When to See a Doctor

A panic attack can feel like a heart attack—racing heart, chest tightness, difficulty breathing, overwhelming fear. While panic attacks themselves aren't dangerous, they're a signal that your body needs attention. Understanding what happens during a panic attack and knowing when to seek professional help can transform how you manage this condition.

Symptoms and Warning Signs

Recognizing the symptoms of panic attacks is the first step toward getting proper treatment. Symptoms can range from mild to severe and may develop gradually or appear suddenly.

It's important not to self-diagnose or self-medicate. A medical professional can differentiate between conditions that may have similar symptoms and ensure you receive the correct treatment for your specific situation.

When to Seek In-Person Care

While online consultations are effective for many conditions, some situations require in-person evaluation. Your online doctor will clearly advise you if your condition requires physical examination, diagnostic tests, or emergency care.

The goal is to provide you with the most appropriate level of care. An online consultation is often the ideal first step—it can save you an unnecessary trip to the clinic or help you understand when in-person care is truly needed.
